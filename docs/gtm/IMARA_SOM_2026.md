# Imara — Bottom-up SOM (2026)

Answers dossier item **M1**: stop sizing the market on the headline gap (a *potential-demand*
abstraction) and build a bottom-up serviceable-obtainable-market from reachable buyers × realistic
conversion × ACV. The gap is quoted only as **context, with its vintage**.

> **Context (not TAM):** SA SME credit gap ≈ **US$5–30bn depending on source** (IFC model ≈US$30bn /
> ≈R509–550bn; SA-specific Finfind/"Unseen Sector" ≈US$5–21bn). 2018–2019 data. Directional, not
> Imara's addressable revenue. (Dossier SA1; sources in the dossier bibliography.)

## The universe Imara actually serves
- **Formal, tax-registered SA SMEs ≈ 250,000** (SBI Baseline Study, SARS data) — *not* the ~2.6m
  total SMMEs (mostly informal, which Imara cannot score today — see M2/alt-data).
- **Only ~5% of formal SMEs access bank credit** (McKinsey/IFC) → the assessment problem is real.
- Imara sells to **the assessors, not the SMEs**: SME lenders/NBFIs, DFIs (SEDFA/IDC/NEF — already
  modelled in `funder_gates.py`), and the **accountant/advisor channel** (the proven SA wedge).

## Bottom-up SOM (illustrative — assumptions stated, all replace-with-real)
SOM = reachable buyers × pilot→paid conversion × annual contract value. Two motions:

**A. Lender / DFI channel (depth)**
- Reachable SA SME lenders + NBFIs + DFIs in 12 months: **~20–40** named targets (the big banks'
  business units, Lula, Merchant Capital, Bridgement, Retail Capital, SEDFA, IDC, NEF, …).
- Pilot→paid conversion (design-partner motion): **~15–25%** → ~3–8 paying lenders.
- ACV per lender (assessment-layer SaaS / per-decision): **~R0.5–2.0m**.
- → **Lender SOM ≈ R2–15m ARR** at this stage.

**B. Accountant / advisor channel (breadth, B2B2SME)**
- SA accounting professionals ≈ **50k SAICA + SAIPA** practitioners; reachable practices in 12 mo:
  **~100–300** firms.
- Conversion to paid seats: **~10–20%**; seats/firm small initially.
- ACV per practice: **~R20–60k**.
- → **Accountant SOM ≈ R0.5–3m ARR** at this stage.

**Combined near-term SOM ≈ R3–18m ARR** — small but *real and defensible*, vs the ~R509bn gap which is
not addressable revenue. The point of M1: present this, with the gap as backdrop.

## Honesty
Every number above marked **illustrative** — they are planning assumptions (reachable-count,
conversion, ACV), **not measured**. The one input that converts assumptions into evidence is the
same Tier-0 unlock: a design partner. Replace each assumption with a real datapoint as pilots land.
Buyer-count and conversion are RUAN-led commercial judgements; ACV firms up at first contract.

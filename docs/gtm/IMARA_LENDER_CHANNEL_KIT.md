# Imara — Lender / NBFI Channel: Design-Partner Shortlist + Outreach Package

**Date:** 22 June 2026
**Goal:** sign one SME lender / NBFI as a design partner to run real applications (including declines) through Imara, generating **labelled approve/repay outcomes** — the fastest path to Score calibration.
**Position (non-negotiable):** Imara is **not a lender** and does not compete for balance sheet. It is the **decision-support / underwriting-intelligence layer** that makes a lender's decisions better, faster, and more defensible — and converts their declines into a future-fundable pipeline.

---

## 1. The channel thesis (why lenders, why now)

- **The market is huge and data-driven.** ~R350bn SME funding gap; the SA alternative-lending market is growing ~13–14%/yr. Lenders already underwrite on data — e.g. **Bridgement plugs into a borrower's accounting software/POS** to assess them in real time; **Lula** decides in 24h up to R5m. They compete on *speed and decision quality*, which is exactly where Imara helps.
- **The lender's two pains Imara removes:** (1) **thin-file / opaque SMEs** they can't confidently underwrite (so they decline good businesses); (2) **explainability + auditability** of decisions (NCA / responsible-lending pressure). Imara gives a deterministic, SA-compliant, *explainable* read (Imara Score + reason codes + Altman-Z′′ distress + bank-signal + fraud flags) with a tamper-evident audit trail.
- **The under-used angle — a decline-to-fundable funnel.** A lender declines most applicants and loses them. Imara turns a decline into a **costed 90-day readiness plan**; the SME comes back fundable. That's more deal flow for the lender from leads they already paid to acquire.

**The pitch in one line:** *Imara is an explainable, SA-compliant underwriting-intelligence layer — a forensic second read on thin-file SMEs, plus a readiness funnel that turns your declines into future-fundable applicants. You keep underwriting; Imara makes the call better and the rejects re-bankable.*

---

## 2. What's in it for the lender (their value proposition)

- **Better decisions on hard files.** A deterministic forensic read (10+ ratios, distress, fraud/anomaly, bank-statement signals) on the SMEs your model is unsure about — a complementary second opinion, not a replacement.
- **Explainable + auditable.** Adverse-action-style reason codes + a hash-chained decision trail — the governance an NCA-regulated lender or a funder's credit committee wants.
- **A pipeline from your declines.** A costed readiness plan re-engages rejected applicants; re-applications convert acquisition spend you'd otherwise write off.
- **Faster onboarding of messy applicants.** Imara turns unstructured financials/bank/tax docs into a structured, scored picture in minutes.
- **White-label / embed option** later — Imara as the intelligence inside your flow, not a competing brand.
- **Deterministic, not LLM-guesswork.** Numbers computed in code and cross-checked; defensible to a regulator (unlike black-box AI scoring).

## 3. Ideal design-partner profile

Best first partner = a **data-forward, mid-sized SME lender open to decision-support** (not the largest incumbents, whose models + procurement are slow):

| Trait | Ideal | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Type | NBFI / fintech SME lender (not a big bank, first) | Faster yes; already data-driven |
| Volume | Enough monthly applications to pilot + generate outcomes | Outcomes are the whole point |
| Decisions | Generates **both** approvals + declines + repayment data | Both label classes needed to calibrate |
| Posture | Open to a second-opinion / readiness tool; not "we have our own model, full stop" | Imara is complementary |
| Reachability | Head of Credit / Risk / Product reachable | Short path to a pilot |
| Regulatory | NCA-registered credit provider | Values explainability + audit |

Avoid: the biggest banks for the *first* partner (procurement + model-risk sign-off are slow) — revisit once you have proof.

## 8. The design-partner pilot offer (lender version)
- **Free**, time-boxed (8–12 weeks).
- **Run a retrospective batch** of recent applications — *including declines* — through Imara; compare the Imara Score/flags to the lender's own decisions and (where available) repayment outcomes. This generates the **labelled approve/repay outcomes** that calibrate the Score.
- Optionally pilot the **readiness funnel** on a cohort of declines.
- **They keep underwriting + the customer.** Imara is decision-support, never the credit decision (NCA).
- **You give:** white-glove integration, the reads, and roadmap input (+ preferential/white-label terms later).
- **Be honest about stage:** the Score isn't yet calibrated against real outcomes — *the pilot earns that, together.* Lenders value a measurable lift vs their baseline; frame success as predictive lift + willingness to embed.

---

## 9. Objection handling
- **"We have our own underwriting model."** → Imara is a *complementary second read* on the files you're unsure about + a funnel for your declines — not a replacement. Measure the lift on a retrospective batch; if it adds no signal, no harm done.
- **"Is the applicants' data safe / POPIA?"** → PII redacted pre-AI; deterministic numbers computed locally; cross-border AI step disclosed + consented; tamper-evident audit log. (Attorney POPIA sign-off in hand.)
- **"Can we trust AI scoring with a regulator?"** → The score is deterministic + explainable (reason codes), cross-checked against an independent distress model — the opposite of black-box. That's *easier* to defend than a pure-ML score.
- **"What does it cost?"** → Pilot is free; you're a design partner shaping it. White-label/commercial terms only if it proves out.

## 10. Two-week execution checklist
**Week 1:** build the lender tracker from section 5; score with the rubric; find warm paths to the top ~8 (Bridgement-type data-forward NBFIs first); prep a sample Imara read to show.
**Week 2:** warm-intro requests first, then cold email + LinkedIn the rest; book calls; on each call run a live read + pitch the retrospective-batch pilot (section 8). **Target: 1 signed lender design partner** (or a retrospective-batch agreement) to start the outcome clock.

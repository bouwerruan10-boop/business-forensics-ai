# Imara — Design-Partner Outreach Kit

**Purpose:** start the *outcome clock* (the v1.23 binding constraint). Everything here is built to land **1 lender/NBFI + 1–2 accountants** as design partners, fast, founder-led. Outcome-based validation can't be shortcut — so the only thing that matters this month is getting Imara in front of real businesses and real decisions.

> Not advice, just assets. Adapt the wording to your voice. Verify any named company's current status before you reach out.

---

## 1. The one-pager (your pitch)

**Imara — make SMEs bankable, make underwriting faster.**

South Africa's SME funding gap is ~**ZAR 550 billion**. Only about **7%** of SMEs get formal bank funding — not because they're all bad risks, but because assessing them is slow, manual, and inconsistent. Lenders drown in incomplete financials; good SMEs get declined or wait weeks.

**What Imara does:** an SME uploads its financials, bank statements and tax/legal docs. Imara returns, in minutes, a structured **bankability report** — deterministic financial forensics (the numbers are computed, not guessed by an AI), SA tax & legal compliance checks, an explainable **Imara Score** with reason codes, an independent **Altman Z′′ distress cross-check**, and a **lender's-eye view** of decline risk. It is **decision-support, not a lender** — it makes the human's call faster, more consistent, and explainable.

**The honest part (and why this is a *design-partner* offer, not a sale):** the Imara Score is a well-built, expert-weighted heuristic that has **not yet been calibrated against real funding/repayment outcomes**. The pilot is how it earns that evidence — *with* you, on *your* deals.

**The pilot (8–12 weeks):**
- You run **20–50 real SMEs** through Imara (applicants you're already assessing).
- Imara produces the report + Score for each; you make your normal decision.
- We record the **outcome** (approved/declined, and later repaid/defaulted) against each Score.
- At the end we **calibrate the Score to your real outcomes** and hand you the discrimination metrics (AUC/Gini) + a model card.

**What you get:** faster pre-screening, a consistent explainable risk read, less manual underwriting, first access, and direct say over the product. Small paid pilot; clear go/kill gates (see `IMARA_PILOT_PROTOCOL.md`).

**What I need from you:** a 20-minute call, and access to a stream of real applications during the pilot.

## 3. Target-partner shortlist (framework + starter list)

**What makes a *good first* design partner (score each 1–5):**
- **Decision volume:** processes enough SME credit decisions to give you ≥30–50 outcomes in a quarter.
- **Outcome access:** willing to share approve/decline now and repay/default later. (This is the single most important filter — outcomes are the asset.)
- **Decision-maker reach:** you can get to the person who says yes (founder/head of credit), not a procurement maze.
- **Right size:** big enough to have volume, small/nimble enough to move in weeks not quarters.
- **Pain fit:** currently doing manual/slow underwriting (lenders) or has fundable-but-stuck clients (accountants).

**Where to find them (sourcing routes):**
- **SA SME fintech lenders / NBFIs** — *verify each is active/relevant before outreach:* Lulalend (Lula), Bridgement, Merchant Capital, Retail Capital, Genfin, Business Partners Ltd, and newer alternative-data lenders. ## 4. Face-validity review packet (the parallel, no-waiting evidence)

While the outcome clock runs, get **2–3 credit/lending professionals** to review a batch of real Imara reports. This is interim *convergent-validity* evidence (best practice = quantitative + expert judgment) you can show partners and investors **before** outcomes accrue.

**Per report, the reviewer answers (1 = strongly disagree, 5 = strongly agree):**

1. The Imara Score band matches my own credit judgment of this business. ☐1 ☐2 ☐3 ☐4 ☐5
2. The reason codes / key findings are valid and material. ☐1 ☐2 ☐3 ☐4 ☐5
3. The report surfaces the issues I'd care about as a lender. ☐1 ☐2 ☐3 ☐4 ☐5
4. Nothing important is wrong or missing. ☐1 ☐2 ☐3 ☐4 ☐5
5. I would act on this report (lend / decline / ask for X). ☐1 ☐2 ☐3 ☐4 ☐5

**Free text:** Where did Imara get it *right*? Where *wrong*? What would you need before trusting the Score?

**Reviewer:** name · role · years in SME credit · date.

**Aggregate it:** mean score per question across reviewers + a list of the specific "wrong/missing" items. That table is your first credibility artifact — and a direct backlog of what to fix.

- **Day 1–2:** fill the tracker (§3) with ~15 names; flag every warm intro.

- **Goal of the fortnight:** **1 pilot verbally agreed** (ideally a lender) + **first face-validity scores in**. That's the clock started.

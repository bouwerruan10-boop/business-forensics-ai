# Imara — Next Steps: The "Responsible Score" workstream
*Research-driven plan. Where Imara is now: live, internally validated (deterministic engine 12/12 correct, LLM-judge 100% human-agreement), data now persists. The gap this plan closes: Imara is internally validated but not yet **lender-grade or responsible-AI-ready** — and that is exactly what its B2B-lender future and the SA regulatory context demand.*

## 1. Research synthesis (two rounds)
**Credit scoring is now high-risk, regulated AI.** The EU AI Act classifies creditworthiness scoring as **high-risk** (explainability, human oversight, risk controls) ([HES](https://hesfintech.com/blog/all-legislative-trends-regulating-ai-in-lending/)). US ECOA/Reg B + CFPB Circular 2023-03 require **specific, accurate principal reasons** for adverse decisions — even from complex AI ([Sei AI](https://www.seiright.com/blog/adverse-action-notices-ai-credit-decisions-ecoa-reg-b), [myFICO](https://www.myfico.com/credit-education/blog/reason-codes)). The dominant technical risk is **bias from proxy variables** — seemingly neutral inputs (zip, occupation, education, phone brand) that correlate with protected traits ([MDPI review](https://www.mdpi.com/1911-8074/19/2/104), [TrustDecision](https://trustdecision.com/articles/how-to-mitigate-ai-discrimination-and-bias-in-financial-services)).

**SME scoring + South Africa.** Traditional models fail SMEs (thin credit history); good SME scoring blends quantitative + qualitative + alternative data ([FasterCapital](https://fastercapital.com/content/SME-credit-risk--Credit-Risk-Assessment-for-SMEs--Key-Factors-and-Best-Practices.html)). SA's **National Credit Act** requires a **pre-agreement affordability assessment** and forbids **reckless lending** (s80); regulators are wary of fully-automated decisions and expect **human review of edge cases**; **POPIA** makes data transparency/control foundational ([SAFLII](https://www.saflii.org/za/journals/PER/2018/52.html), [ACPAS](https://www.acpas.co.za/Reckless-lending.html)).

**The standard artifacts.** **Reason codes** = score factors listed most-impactful-first, consistent with the *actual* model (not a surrogate). **Fairness testing** = Information-Value proxy detection + ongoing disparate-impact tests, not a one-off. **Model cards** = lightweight "nutrition labels" (intended use, where NOT to use, metrics, ethical caveats) — now foundational, not optional ([Credo AI](https://www.credo.ai/glossary/model-card), [Mitchell et al. 2019]).

## 2. Questions → answers (from the research)
- **Q1 Reason codes?** Imara already has weighted score components + per-finding flags. It can emit **deterministic reason codes**: rank components by their `weight × (100 − value)` contribution to the score shortfall and state the specific driver ("Profitability is the largest drag — net margin 0.9% vs sector 7%"). Honest (mirrors the real math), lender-grade, no API.
- **Q2 Bias?** **Yes, a concrete issue exists:** `bbbee_level` (a race-linked attribute) flows through the SA Legal agent into `sa_legal_risk_score` → the **Legal Compliance component (0.05 weight)** of the Imara Score. A low BBBEE level can therefore *lower* the bankability score. Needs a deliberate, documented fix (make BBBEE **informational / commercial-opportunity context**, not a score penalty) + a proxy check in the eval.
- **Q3 NCA positioning?** The Score must be explicit **decision-support**, not a credit decision — it *supports* a lender's affordability assessment, keeps a human in the loop, and must not be presented as a creditworthiness determination. Partly present (MethodologyNote) but should be formalized.
- **Q4 Model card?** Yes — cheapest, highest-trust governance artifact for the lender audience and SA regulators; documents intended use, the deterministic+LLM method, eval results, limitations (uncalibrated vs outcomes), and the NCA/POPIA framing.

## 3. The plan (prioritized; most is buildable NOW — deterministic, no outcome data needed)
1. **★ Score reason codes / explainability (BUILD FIRST).** Deterministic `reason_codes(report)` from the actual Imara components — ordered principal drivers (negative *and* positive), each tied to the specific ratio/finding behind it. Surface in the report + dashboard + PDF. Turns "a number" into "an explained number" and satisfies adverse-action-style explainability. *No API, fully testable.*
2. **Fairness & proxy audit + fix.** Confirm and remove the BBBEE-as-penalty path: make BBBEE level **informational** (a commercial-access insight) rather than a Score input, or document an explicit neutral rationale. Add an Information-Value-style proxy check to the eval harness, and a standing "fairness notes" section. *Surfaces and closes a real bias vector.*
3. **Model card (governance doc).** `MODEL_CARD.md` + a `/api/v1/model-card` (or report field): intended use = decision-support; where NOT to use; method; eval baselines (deterministic 12/12, judge 100%); limitations; NCA/POPIA framing. *Cheap, high-trust, B2B-ready.*
4. **NCA decision-support guardrails.** Formalize the "not a credit decision; supports the affordability assessment; human-in-the-loop" framing in the report/exports and the public Score contract. *Small copy/flag change, real risk reduction.*

**Deferred (needs real data — unchanged):** Layer-3 Score **calibration** against real funding/repayment outcomes, and true **disparate-impact testing** (both require accumulated real analyses + outcomes — now possible thanks to the persistence fix).

**Sequencing rationale:** (1) is the biggest credibility win and self-contained; (2) closes a concrete bias vector and should ship close behind; (3)+(4) are cheap documentation/framing that make Imara presentable to a lender and defensible to a regulator. All preserve Imara's deterministic, anti-hallucination DNA, and none are blocked on data — unlike calibration.

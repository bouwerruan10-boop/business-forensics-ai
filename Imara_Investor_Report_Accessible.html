The opportunity

Imara is an AI-powered business-intelligence and bankability platform built for South African small and medium enterprises (SMEs). A business uploads its financials, bank statements, tax and legal documents; Imara runs a forensic, multi-agent analysis and returns a single, explainable verdict on the firm's health and fundability — together with a costed plan to improve it.

South Africa has a structural SME-finance problem and a fast-growing appetite to solve it with data. Lenders increasingly underwrite on artificial intelligence and alternative data, but the SMEs themselves — and the accountants and advisers around them — lack a trustworthy, jurisdiction-specific engine that turns messy documents into a lender-ready, defensible picture. Imara is that decision-support layer: it makes SMEs bankable and makes the institutions assessing them faster and smarter.

The problem: why good SMEs still cannot get funded

South Africa's small businesses generate roughly a third of GDP and most of its jobs, yet only a sliver can access formal credit. The barrier is rarely that the business is unviable — it is that its financial story is opaque, unstructured, and hard to verify. Lenders see thin files, inconsistent management accounts, and unquantified risk, and decline by default.

Three forces make this the moment to fix it:

  • A persistent funding gap. An estimated R350 billion of unmet SME demand; micro-firms — most of the applicants and job-creators — are the least likely to be approved.
  • A pivot to AI underwriting. South Africa's alternative-lending market is growing double-digits, and the industry is shifting to AI and alternative-data credit scoring.
  • A trust problem with AI. Generic large-language-model tools hallucinate numbers and miss local law, which is unacceptable in finance. The winner must be verifiable and jurisdiction-aware.
  • An under-served middle. Between do-it-yourself spreadsheets and elite advisory sits a vast band of SMEs and their accountants with no AI-native, South-Africa-specific intelligence engine.

How Imara works: one upload in, a full diagnosis out

Imara combines a deterministic financial engine with a team of specialist AI agents. The numbers — ratios, scores, savings, forecasts — are computed in code and are fully traceable. The AI agents interpret and narrate those numbers in the language of each business domain, and every narrative claim is automatically checked back against the computed facts. The result is the fluency of AI with the discipline of an audit.

A single analysis runs up to about twenty specialist passes in minutes and produces:

  • A bankability verdict — the Imara Score, a 0 to 100 rating and an A to E band, with the precise reasons behind it.
  • Quantified findings — every issue carries a rand impact, a return-on-investment estimate, a cost of inaction, and a benchmark reference.
  • A 90-day roadmap — a prioritised, costed action plan, plus quick wins actionable in under 30 days.
  • An interactive what-if simulator — re-computes the score and cash position as the owner models decisions.

Built deterministic-first, Imara's core numbers are immune to manipulation: text injected into an uploaded document can never move the computed ratios or the score.

What Imara can do: platform capabilities

Imara is not a single feature — it is a full analytical suite already built and running in production.

Imara Score: bankability in one number

A penalty-weighted score from 0 to 100 plus an A to E band, with weights set by an expert analytic-hierarchy-process model and adverse-action-style "why this score" reason codes. It is cross-checked against the independent Altman Z-double-prime distress model for convergent validity.

Forensic multi-agent analysis

More than seventeen specialist agents — covering finance, accounting, audit, operations, logistics, sales, marketing, human resources, procurement, strategy, and legal risk — each producing quantified, benchmarked findings, orchestrated into one coherent narrative and roadmap.

South African compliance intelligence

Tax compliance (value-added tax, income tax, employee taxes, provisional tax, and tax clearance) and corporate law (the Companies Act, broad-based black economic empowerment, the Protection of Personal Information Act, labour law, consumer and credit law, and company-registry compliance) — grounded in a dated, citation-tagged legal corpus so findings cite real Acts and sections.

Tax Me If You Can: legal tax optimisation

Surfaces reliefs an SME qualifies for but may be missing — such as small-business-corporation tax rates, the employment tax incentive, and accelerated allowances — with quantified savings, respecting anti-avoidance rules and flagged for professional sign-off.

Decision-support suite

An action simulator with Monte Carlo ranges and biggest-lever sensitivity; a macro-economics overlay that stress-tests interest-rate, currency, inflation and electricity shocks on the specific firm; supplier-savings benchmarking; a lender's-eye-view decline-risk read; funding-fit product matching; and bank-statement intelligence.

Trust and governance by design

A deterministic-first, anti-hallucination architecture with an automatic faithfulness check on every cited figure and a narrative-consistency verifier; a published model card; a tamper-evident, hash-chained audit log; data handling aligned to the Protection of Personal Information Act; and operator authentication.

Production-grade platform

Live on cloud infrastructure, continuously tested with more than 270 automated checks, security- and compliance-hardened, with backups, observability and a tamper-evident decision trail.

Why Imara wins: differentiation

  • Deterministic-first, not language-model guesswork. Competitors let a language model produce the numbers, and inherit its hallucinations. Imara computes every figure in code and uses AI only to explain, then verifies the explanation.
  • Built for South African law. A current, dated, citable corpus of South African tax and company law — not a United States tool with a local skin. Findings reference the actual Act and section.
  • An intelligence layer, not a lender. Imara does not compete with banks or fintech lenders for balance sheet; it makes their decisions better and SMEs more fundable. That makes lenders and accountants channels, not rivals.
  • Defensible and auditable. Explainable scores, a model card, and a hash-chained audit trail — the governance a regulator or credit committee asks for, already in place.

A large, growing, AI-ready market

Imara sits at the intersection of two expanding curves: a stubborn R350 billion SME funding gap, with only about 5 percent of formal micro-firms able to access credit; and a South African alternative-lending market growing roughly 13 to 14 percent a year toward about 1.9 billion US dollars by 2029, underpinned by a decisive industry shift to AI and alternative-data credit scoring. More than 300 funders now offer over 600 products, yet the SMEs and advisers in the middle still lack a trustworthy engine to make a business fundable in the first place. That gap is Imara's wedge.

Where we are, and where this goes

Imara is built and live in production — a complete, hardened platform delivering a full forensic analysis in minutes, with the trust, governance and South-African depth that the category demands. The engineering is mature; the next chapter is reach and evidence.

  1. Now — a complete product. The full analytical suite, score, compliance, optimisation, simulation and governance are built, tested and deployed.
  2. Next — design-partner pilots. Deploy with a lender and a cohort of accountants to put Imara into real funding decisions and capture outcome data.
  3. Then — an outcome-calibrated score. Tune the Imara Score against real funding and repayment outcomes, turning a strong engine into an evidence-backed standard.
  4. Scale — channel and white-label. Grow through the accountant and adviser channel, and as embedded underwriting intelligence for lenders across the region.

Imara turns the hardest part of SME finance — trust in the numbers — into software. The product is here; the opportunity is the market it unlocks.

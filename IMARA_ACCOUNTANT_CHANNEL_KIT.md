# Imara — Accountant Channel: Design-Partner Shortlist + Outreach Package

**Date:** 22 June 2026
**Goal:** sign 1–3 SA accounting/advisory firms as design partners to run real SME clients through Imara, start the outcome clock, and gather face-validity feedback.
**Why this channel:** accountants already hold SME trust and the financial data — the most under-used early distribution channel. This package is built to be executed, not just read.

---

## 1. The channel thesis (why accountants, why now)

Three forces make SA accountants the right first channel for Imara:

- **Accountants are becoming advisors.** The fastest-growing part of the SA accounting sector is advisory/consulting, and firms are adopting cloud + AI to scale advisory delivery. "Accountants today double as business advisors." Imara is advisory-in-a-box.
- **The profession is being pushed toward loan-readiness.** CIBA (the SME-focused body) issued **Practice Note 01/2026 with a 12-point loan-readiness checklist** and is steering members into funding-access advisory. Accountants are being *told* to do this work — Imara is the tool that does it in minutes.
- **The pain Imara removes is the #1 reason SME funding fails.** Industry guidance is explicit: **"weak or incomplete documentation is one of the most common reasons applications fail."** Imara turns a client's messy financials into a lender-ready, defensible, scored picture — exactly the gap.

**The pitch in one line:** *Imara lets you offer your SME clients a fundability / loan-readiness service — a scored, explainable, SA-compliant diagnosis in minutes — turning compliance work into advisory revenue, without becoming a lender or losing the client relationship.*

---

## 2. What's in it for the accountant (their value proposition)

Lead with their upside, not Imara's features:

- **A new advisory revenue line.** Package "fundability reviews" / "loan-readiness reports" as a paid service on top of compliance work — the advisory shift their body is pushing them toward.
- **Makes their clients more fundable.** A costed 90-day plan + Imara Score gives the client a concrete path and the accountant a reason for an ongoing advisory conversation.
- **Scales their judgment with AI — deterministic, not guesswork.** Numbers are computed in code (traceable), the AI only narrates, and every figure is cross-checked. It augments the accountant; it doesn't replace their sign-off.
- **SA-specific and defensible.** Cites real Acts/sections (Companies Act, VAT, POPIA, B-BBEE), with a model card and audit trail — credible to put in front of a client or a bank.
- **No conflict, no risk to the relationship.** Imara is decision-support, **not a lender** — the accountant keeps the client and the trust; Imara is their tool.

## 3. Ideal design-partner profile (who to target)

Score candidates against this; the best early partner is a **tech-forward, advisory-minded, owner-led SME practice**:

| Trait | Ideal | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Size | 2–20 professionals (small–mid practice) | Big enough to have a client base, small enough that the owner can say "yes" fast |
| Client base | 20–300 active SMEs | Enough volume to pilot + generate outcomes; SME (not large-corp) focus |
| Software | On Xero / Sage / QuickBooks cloud | Tech-forward, clean data, comfortable adopting a new tool |
| Posture | Already offers (or wants to offer) advisory / loan-readiness | Imara slots into an existing motion, not a cold sell |
| Body | CIBA or SAIPA member (SME-focused); SAICA fine too | Aligned to the loan-readiness push; designations signal credibility |
| Decision-maker | Owner / partner reachable directly | Short path to a pilot decision |
| Geography | Start Gauteng / Western Cape (density), then national | Easier face-to-face; most firms cluster there |

**Decision-maker = the firm owner/partner.** Avoid large multinationals (Big 4 / mid-tier networks) for the *first* partner — slow procurement; revisit once you have proof.

## 4. Partner-scoring rubric (0–10; prioritise 7+)

Score each candidate, pursue highest first:

- **Advisory intent (0–3):** already markets advisory / loan-readiness / CFO services?
- **SME fit (0–2):** clearly SME-focused client base (not audit-of-large-corp)?
- **Tech-forward (0–2):** Xero/Sage/QuickBooks certified / cloud-first?
- **Reachability (0–2):** owner/partner directly contactable (named, email/LinkedIn)?
- **Warm path (0–1):** any mutual connection for a warm intro?

## 8. The design-partner pilot offer (mechanics)

Keep the ask light and the value obvious:

- **Free**, time-boxed (8–12 weeks).
- **They run ~5–15 of their SME clients** through Imara (with client consent — POPIA matters).
- **They review each report** and tell you: is the verdict right? are the findings useful? would you put this in front of a bank? (face-validity feedback — your interim evidence).
- **Over the pilot, capture outcomes** where possible (did flagged clients improve / get funded?) — this starts the outcome clock for Score calibration.
- **They keep the client relationship and the sign-off.** Imara is their tool.
- **You give them:** white-glove onboarding, the reports, and early input into the roadmap (+ preferential terms later).
- **Be honest about stage:** Imara is a complete, live platform, but the Score isn't yet calibrated against real funding outcomes — *that's what the pilot earns together.* Design partners value being early, not being sold a finished thing.

## 9. Objection handling

- **"Is my clients' data safe?"** → POPIA-aligned; PII redacted before any AI step; document text processed by a US AI service is disclosed and consented; deterministic numbers computed locally. (Get your own attorney's POPIA sign-off before the pilot — it's drafted.)
- **"I already do this manually."** → Exactly — Imara does the 8-hour version in minutes, with SA-law citations and a defensible audit trail, so you can offer it to *more* clients as a productised service.
- **"Can I trust the AI's numbers?"** → The numbers aren't the AI's — they're computed in code and cross-checked; the AI only narrates. Show the faithfulness check.
- **"What does it cost / what's the catch?"** → The pilot is free; you're a design partner shaping the product. No lock-in.
- **"I don't have time."** → 20-minute call; you pick 5 clients; we onboard you. Minimal lift, new advisory revenue if it lands.
